India, South Asia

Depending on the State, India has a malnutrition rate of 15%-30%. With around 194.4 Million people not receiving nutrition, India is the largest contributor of undernourished people. India contributes a third of the global burden of undernutrition.

Malnutrition in India mainly happens because of the economic inequality and caste-system. Malnutrition and its effects can also be partially linked to the lack of political attention given to the budgetary provisions made to address this problem.
